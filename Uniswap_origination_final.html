Introduction¶

Uniswap, as one of the leading decentralized exchanges in the blockchain ecosystem, offers users various avenues for interacting with its pools. While many users prefer the Uniswap UI for its ease of use, the open nature of the blockchain enables multiple alternative approaches for trading. These include the use of aggregators that act as intermediaries for token swaps and direct interaction with the Uniswap contract, as observed in the activities of most Miner Extractable Value (MEV) bots.

The primary objective of this dashboard is to conduct a comprehensive analysis of user origination across Uniswap, focusing on volume and user count distribution among different platforms used for trading. To facilitate a deeper understanding of the various pool dynamics, we categorize users into distinct groups, namely:

  • Uniswap Frontend: Users who utilize the Uniswap interface directly.
  • MEV: Users engaging in trading through Miner Extractable Value strategies.
  • Aggregators: Users employing aggregator services for token swaps.
  • Unknown Others: Users falling into a category that requires further investigation to uncover insights about their trading behavior.

Methodology¶

I will leverage data from the ethereum.core.ez_dex_swaps and ethereum.core.dim_labels tables provided by Flipsidecrypto to gain insights into user origination on Uniswap.

To understand the user origination, we need to consider the sender of each swap transaction. If the sender is different from the origin_from_address or does not appear to be associated with Uniswap, we can rely on the label field from the dim_labels table to determine the transaction's source. On the other hand, if the sender is the same as the origin_from_address or is directly related to Uniswap, it indicates that the transaction originates directly from the Uniswap interface.

Our analysis will focus on the following key points:

  • Identifying User Origination: By comparing the sender with the origin_from_address and examining the label field, we will categorize transactions into two groups: those originating from Uniswap's frontend interface and those from other platforms or sources, facilitated by aggregators, MEV bots, or other interfaces.

  • Understanding User Behavior: We will explore the volume and user count for each user group to gauge their impact on Uniswap's trading ecosystem. This analysis will help identify which user groups contribute the most to Uniswap's overall activity.

  • Characteristics of User Groups: We will examine distribution patterns and key metrics, such as average and median swaps, for each user group. This will provide insights into the trading behaviors and preferences of different user segments.
